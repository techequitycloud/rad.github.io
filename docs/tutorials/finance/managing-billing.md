# Managing Billing and Invoicing

Welcome to the RAD Platform tutorial for finance users. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the billing, invoicing, and revenue tracking features available in the platform. You will learn how to access financial reports, manage customer invoices, and monitor revenue streams.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you will be able to:
- Access and interpret the financial dashboard.
- Generate and customize billing reports.
- Manage customer invoices and payment statuses.
- Track revenue from module usage and subscriptions.
- Understand the process for reconciling payments.

## Prerequisites

- You must have a user account with "Finance" role permissions.
- You should have a basic understanding of accounting principles.

## Estimated Time to Complete

This tutorial will take approximately 25 minutes to complete.

## Step-by-Step Guide

### 1. Accessing the Financial Dashboard

The financial dashboard is your central hub for all billing and revenue information.

- **Log in** to the RAD Platform.
- Navigate to the **"Finance"** section from the main menu.
- The dashboard displays key metrics, including total revenue, outstanding invoices, and recent transactions.

### 2. Generating Billing Reports

You can generate detailed billing reports for specific time periods or customer segments.

- From the financial dashboard, click on the **"Reports"** tab.
- Select the desired report type (e.g., "Monthly Revenue," "Customer Invoices").
- Use the filters to specify the date range, customer, or module.
- Click **"Generate Report"** to view the report. You can also download it as a CSV file for offline analysis.

### 3. Managing Customer Invoices

The platform automates invoice generation, but you can also manage invoices manually.

- Go to the **"Invoices"** tab in the Finance section.
- You will see a list of all generated invoices, along with their status (e.g., "Paid," "Unpaid," "Overdue").
- To view an invoice, click on the invoice number.
- You can also manually create a new invoice, send reminders, or record offline payments.

### 4. Tracking Revenue

The platform provides tools for tracking revenue from different sources.

- In the **"Revenue"** tab, you can view a breakdown of revenue by module, customer, or subscription plan.
- Use the charts and graphs to visualize revenue trends over time.
- This information can help you identify popular modules and profitable customer segments.

### 5. Reconciling Payments

The platform integrates with popular payment gateways to automate payment reconciliation.

- In the **"Transactions"** tab, you can view a list of all payment transactions.
- The system automatically matches payments to invoices.
- If there are any discrepancies, you can manually reconcile the payments.

## Verification

After completing this tutorial, you should be able to:
- Confidently navigate the financial dashboard.
- Generate a billing report for the last quarter.
- Find and view a specific customer invoice.
- Explain the revenue breakdown for the current month.

## Troubleshooting

- **"I can't see the 'Finance' section."**
  - This is likely a permissions issue. Contact your platform administrator to ensure your account has the "Finance" role.
- **"A report is not generating."**
  - Check the date range and filters you have applied. If the issue persists, contact support.
- **"An invoice is showing an incorrect amount."**
  - Verify the usage data for the module associated with the invoice. If you still believe the amount is incorrect, contact support for assistance.

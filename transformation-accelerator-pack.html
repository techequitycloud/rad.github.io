Session Design: 60 + 60 Format
First 60 Minutes — Framework & Evidence

Theory, Research & Case Study Discussion

Each session opens with the research evidence (DORA data, Gartner forecasts, Google best practice), a framework explanation, and a facilitated discussion mapping the concepts to the organisation’s current state. Participants understand the “why” before the “how.”

Second 60 Minutes — Hands-On RAD Lab

Real Infrastructure. Real GCP Console. Real Outcomes.

Every session deploys or configures real infrastructure in the participant’s GCP environment via the RAD platform. Participants observe outcomes in the GCP console, connect configuration decisions to certification exam objectives, and leave with running systems — not screenshots.

Measurable Outcomes by Design

Programs begin with a DORA baseline assessment and end with a re-measurement. Every engagement produces a DORA improvement report, a Well-Architected Review finding, and a certification pathway progress record — giving clients and partners quantifiable evidence of transformation ROI.
